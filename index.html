The Life of a Caterpillar

Egg Stage

In the spring during the months of February and early March, the previous generation of butterflies come out of hibernation to find a mate. Once they find a mate, the eggs are laid. Monarch butterflies lay their eggs on the undersides of milkweed leaves.

The egg is oval-shaped (much like that of a football) with little tiny lines of dots running down the sides. The egg takes between three to five days to hatch.

CaterPillar

Caterpillars only eat one type of leaf, so it is important that the eggs are laid on the right type of leaf. Monarch Butterfly caterpillars eat milkweed leaves.

Caterpillars need to eat a lot so they can grow quickly in order to turn into a butterfly.

Cocoon Stage

After the caterpillar sheds it's skin for the last time, it enters the pupa stage. The caterpillar's skin becomes enveloped in a jade green casing called the chrysalis. This skin is about and inch thick and within an hour of being created, it hardens into a protective shell. It takes the caterpillar around 9 to 14 days to transform and once it does, the chrysalis cracks open and out comes a butterfly!

ButterFly Stage

About an hour after emerging from its chrysalis, the monarch butterfly's wings are ready for flying. The butterfly uses its straw-like tongue (called a proboscis) to sip nectar from flowers. Did You Know?

  • The average lifespan of a butterfly is 12 months
  • Butterflies cannot fly if their body temperature is less than 86 degrees.
  • Monarch butterflies journey from the Great Lakes to the Gulf of Mexico (a distance of about 2,000 miles), returning to the North again in the spring.
